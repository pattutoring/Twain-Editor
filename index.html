Write like Mark Twain...

Twain’s Timeless Writing Rules:

  • Never use a five-dollar word when a fifty-cent word will do.
  • Use plain, simple language, short words, and brief sentences.
  • Be cautious with adjectives.
  • Substitute “damn” for “very.”
  • Choose the precise word, not its approximation.
  • Avoid ending sentences with prepositions.
  • Avoid clichés, comparisons, and exaggeration.
  • Be specific and avoid redundancy.
  • Never generalize or ask rhetorical questions.
  • Trim the fluff—simplicity is the soul of good writing.
